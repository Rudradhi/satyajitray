About The Movie

"Aparajito" (1956), directed by Satyajit Ray, is the second film in the acclaimed Apu Trilogy, following "Pather Panchali." The film continues the story of Apu, a young boy from a rural Bengali family, who moves to the city of Benares with his mother after his father's death. It explores Apu's growth, his thirst for education, and the resulting strain on his relationship with his mother. Ray's poignant narrative and sensitive portrayal of human emotions, complemented by Subrata Mitra's evocative cinematography and Ravi Shankar's music, make "Aparajito" a significant work in world cinema.

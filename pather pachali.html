About The Movie

"Pather Panchali" is a 1955 Bengali film directed by Satyajit Ray, based on Bibhutibhushan Bandyopadhyay's novel. It follows Apu, a young boy growing up in a poor village in rural Bengal. The film explores the struggles of Apu's family, including his optimistic but unsuccessful father, resilient mother, and beloved sister, Durga, whose death deeply affects them. Celebrated for its realistic depiction of rural life and emotional depth, "Pather Panchali" marked Ray's directorial debut and won international acclaim, including an award at the 1956 Cannes Film Festival.
